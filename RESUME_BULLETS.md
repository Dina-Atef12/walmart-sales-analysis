## Interview Talking Points

Prepare to discuss these insights in interviews:

### "Tell me about your most complex analysis"
**Response**: "I analyzed 45 Walmart stores' sales data over 143 weeks. The interesting challenge wasn't just finding patterns — it was translating them into business value. For example, I discovered that holiday weeks average 7.8% higher sales, but the real insight was *when* that uplift happens. It's Week 44 (late October), not the holiday itself. That means inventory pre-positioning, not just holiday inventory. That's the kind of insight that changes operations."

### "How do you approach a new dataset?"
**Response**: "I follow a structured workflow: First, understand the structure (shape, types, missing values) — this is your foundation. Second, explore distributions and basic statistics. Third, look for relationships and patterns. Fourth, validate hypotheses statistically. Finally, translate technical findings into business language. In the Walmart project, I used correlation analysis to discover unemployment was a demand signal, but I could only make that actionable by showing how it varies by region and timing."

### "Walk me through a key finding"
**Response**: "One finding was store concentration risk. The top 20% of stores generate 31% of revenue. Sounds like a problem, but the business interpretation depends on variance. I used KMeans clustering to segment stores into tiers — Tier 1 (high, stable), Tier 2 (mid-tier), Tier 3 (emerging). This let me recommend tier-specific playbooks: premium investment for Tier 1, operational efficiency for Tier 3, best-practice sharing across tiers. Same data, but much more actionable."

### "What tools do you use for analysis?"
**Response**: "I use Python with Pandas for data manipulation, NumPy for numeric operations, and SciPy for statistical tests. For visualization, I prefer Seaborn and Matplotlib for publication-quality static charts, and Plotly for interactive dashboards. For this project, I used all of these to create both static executive reports and interactive exploration tools. I also use Jupyter notebooks because they blend code, narrative, and visualization — perfect for communicating analysis."

### "How do you ensure quality?"
**Response**: "Data quality is the foundation. In this project, I profiled the dataset (using pandas-profiling), audited missing values (found zero), flagged outliers (1.02% — retained for signal), and checked for duplicates. I documented all assumptions, including that I flagged rather than dropped outliers because Black Friday surges are real signal, not noise. Finally, I validated statistical tests (checked assumptions) and made my methodology transparent in the executive summary."
